OVERVIEW
--------
Med-3 is a 16-bed general internal medicine ward operating 24 hours a day,
7 days a week. The ward employs registered nurses (sjuksköterskor) and
specialist physicians (läkare). Night nursing coverage is supplemented by
a hospital-wide nursing pool for overflow situations.

SHIFT DEFINITIONS
-----------------
Shift   Code   Start   End    Duration   Description
-----   ----   -----   ---    --------   -----------
Day     D      07:00   15:00   8 h       Main ward shift. Full clinical activity.
Evening E      15:00   23:00   8 h       Reduced staffing. Admission and evening rounds.
Night   N      23:00   07:00   8 h       Minimal staffing. Monitoring + acute care only.

All shifts include a 30-minute unpaid break (per Arbetstidslagen §15).
Effective clinical hours per shift: 7.5 hours.


MINIMUM STAFFING REQUIREMENTS (Hard Operational Constraints)
-------------------------------------------------------------
These are the minimum headcounts that MUST be met. The scheduler treats
these as non-negotiable hard constraints (Tier 2).

WEEKDAYS (Monday–Friday):
  Shift       Nurses (min)   Doctors (min)   Notes
  ---------   ------------   -------------   -----
  Day         2              1               Patient-to-nurse ratio on 16-bed ward
  Evening     1              0               On-call doctor covers (see below)
  Night       1              0               On-call covers; nursing pool available

WEEKENDS (Saturday–Sunday):
  Shift       Nurses (min)   Doctors (min)   Notes
  ---------   ------------   -------------   -----
  Day         2              1               Reduced but must maintain medical supervision
  Evening     1              0               On-call doctor covers
  Night       1              0               Nursing pool supplement available

PREFERRED STAFFING (Soft Constraints — scheduler should aim for but cannot guarantee):
  Weekday Day:       4 nurses,  1 doctor  (ideal for patient load)
  Weekday Evening:   3 nurses,  0 doctors
  Night (all days):  2 nurses,  0 doctors
  Weekend Day:       3 nurses,  1 doctor

NOTE: With 8 nurses at varying contracts, preferred targets should be achievable
on most weekdays. The solver will maximise coverage subject to law constraints
and individual contract hours.


DOCTOR ON-CALL COVERAGE
------------------------
Doctors not scheduled for a ward shift are expected to be available for
telephone consultation (beredskap, not beredskap at the ward).
Weekend on-call rotation is managed separately and is NOT part of this schedule.
The minimum doctor requirement above reflects ward-present doctors only.
With 4 doctors, at least 1 can normally cover the ward on any given day.


SHIFT ROTATION POLICY
---------------------
To promote fairness and work-life balance, the scheduler should distribute
shift types equitably across all eligible staff over the 8-week horizon.

Guidelines (soft constraints):
  - No nurse should work more than 4 consecutive evening shifts.
  - No nurse should work more than 3 consecutive night shifts.
  - Shift rotation direction: forward rotation is preferred
    (Day → Evening → Night) when shifts change, not backward rotation.
    Backward rotation (Night → Evening → Day) is harder on circadian rhythm.
  - Night shift workers should have at least 2 days off after the last night shift.


CONTRACT HOURS
--------------
Staff member hours are defined in their individual configuration files.
The scheduler must not exceed each employee's contracted weekly hours.
Under-scheduling relative to contract is tracked and flagged in the report.

Standard working week: 5 × 8-hour shifts = 40 hours/week (100% FTE)
Part-time equivalents:
  80%  FTE = 4 shifts/week (32 h/week)
  75%  FTE = 3-4 shifts/week (30 h/week)
  60%  FTE = 3 shifts/week (24 h/week)
  50%  FTE = 2-3 shifts/week (20 h/week)


ANNUAL LEAVE AND PLANNED ABSENCES
-----------------------------------
Planned vacation and specific days off are declared in individual staff
configuration files. These are treated as hard constraints and cannot
be overridden by staffing minimums. If minimum staffing cannot be met
due to simultaneous absences, the system will flag an infeasibility.


PUBLIC HOLIDAYS WITHIN PLANNING HORIZON
-----------------------------------------
If the planning horizon includes Swedish public holidays (helgdagar),
they should be treated as Sundays for staffing purposes. Weekend minimums apply.
Public holidays in the April–June 2026 period:
  2026-05-01  Första maj (Labour Day)          — falls in planning window
  2026-05-14  Kristi himmelsfärd (Ascension)   — falls in planning window
  2026-05-24  Pingstdagen (Whit Sunday)        — falls in planning window

Apply weekend staffing minimums on these dates regardless of weekday.


REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------
At completion of each scheduling cycle the system must produce:
  1. Per-staff .ics calendar file (importable into Outlook / Google Calendar)
  2. Admin overview in Excel (.xlsx) with:
     - Week-by-week grid (dates as columns, staff as rows)
     - Colour-coded cells by shift type
     - Summary row: total shifts scheduled per staff vs. contracted
     - Compliance column: flags any law-constraint violations (must show none)
  3. LLM-generated narrative report explaining:
     - Any preferences that could not be honoured and why
     - Any near-misses or trade-offs
     - Infeasibility warnings if minimum staffing was not achievable on any day
