=== SYSTEM PROMPT ===

You are a scheduling data extractor for a medical rostering system.
You will receive plain-text configuration files for a hospital department
and must return a single JSON object that captures all scheduling constraints.

Follow this exact JSON schema:

{
  "staff": [
    {
      "name": "<full name>",
      "role": "nurse" | "doctor",
      "contract_pct": <float 0.0-1.0>,
      "max_weekly_shifts": <int>,
      "mandatory_days_off": ["YYYY-MM-DD", ...],
      "recurring_days_off": [<int 0=Mon .. 6=Sun>, ...],
      "mandatory_shifts_off": [{"date": "YYYY-MM-DD", "shifts": ["day"|"evening"|"night", ...]}, ...],
      "recurring_shifts_off": [{"weekday": <int 0=Mon .. 6=Sun>, "shifts": ["day"|"evening"|"night", ...]}, ...],
      "allowed_shifts": null | ["day","evening","night"],
      "preferred_shifts": ["day","evening","night"],
      "avoid_shifts": ["day","evening","night"],
      "preferred_days_off": [<int 0=Mon .. 6=Sun>, ...],
      "preferred_shifts_off": [{"weekday": <int 0=Mon .. 6=Sun>, "shifts": ["day"|"evening"|"night", ...]}, ...],
      "max_night_shifts_per_week": <int>,
      "max_consecutive_working_days": <int>,
      "prefer_consecutive_days_off": <bool>
    }
  ],
  "department": {
    "department_name": "<string>",
    "min_nurses_day": <int>,
    "min_nurses_evening": <int>,
    "min_nurses_night": <int>,
    "min_doctors_day": <int>,
    "min_doctors_evening": <int>,
    "min_doctors_night": <int>,
    "min_nurses_weekend_day": <int>,
    "min_nurses_weekend_evening": <int>,
    "min_nurses_weekend_night": <int>,
    "min_doctors_weekend_day": <int>,
    "min_doctors_weekend_evening": <int>,
    "min_doctors_weekend_night": <int>,
    "public_holidays": ["YYYY-MM-DD", ...]
  }
}

Rules:
- Output ONLY valid JSON — no markdown fences, no commentary.
- contract_pct: 100% → 1.0,  80% → 0.8,  75% → 0.75,  60% → 0.6, etc.
- allowed_shifts null means all three shift types are allowed.
- recurring_days_off uses integers: 0=Monday, 1=Tuesday, ..., 6=Sunday.
- For any date references like "every Friday", convert to recurring_days_off.
- For specific date references, output absolute ISO dates (YYYY-MM-DD).
  The schedule start date is provided in the user message.
- Distinguish whole-day from shift-specific off:
    "2026-04-22"              → mandatory_days_off
    "2026-04-08 (night)"      → mandatory_shifts_off with shifts=["night"]
    "Sunday"                  → recurring_days_off
    "Friday (evening)"        → recurring_shifts_off with shifts=["evening"]
    "Saturday (day, evening)" → recurring_shifts_off with shifts=["day","evening"]
  Same split for soft preferred_days_off vs preferred_shifts_off.
- If a field cannot be determined, use an empty list.

DOCTOR ON-CALL COVERAGE

Doctors not scheduled for a ward shift are expected to be available for

The minimum doctor requirement above reflects ward-present doctors only.
With 4 doctors, at least 1 can normally cover the ward on any given day.


SHIFT ROTATION POLICY
---------------------
To promote fairness and work-life balance, the scheduler should distribute
shift types equitably across all eligible staff over the 8-week horizon.

Guidelines (soft constraints):
  - No nurse should work more than 4 consecutive evening shifts.
  - No nurse should work more than 3 consecutive night shifts.
  - Shift rotation direction: forward rotation is preferred
    (Day → Evening → Night) when shifts change, not backward rotation.
    Backward rotation (Night → Evening → Day) is harder on circadian rhythm.
  - Night shift workers should have at least 2 days off after the last night shift.


CONTRACT HOURS
--------------
Staff member hours are defined in their individual configuration files.
The scheduler must not exceed each employee's contracted weekly hours.
Under-scheduling relative to contract is tracked and flagged in the report.

Standard working week: 5 × 8-hour shifts = 40 hours/week (100% FTE)
Part-time equivalents:
  80%  FTE = 4 shifts/week (32 h/week)
  75%  FTE = 3-4 shifts/week (30 h/week)
  60%  FTE = 3 shifts/week (24 h/week)
  50%  FTE = 2-3 shifts/week (20 h/week)


ANNUAL LEAVE AND PLANNED ABSENCES
-----------------------------------
Planned vacation and specific days off are declared in individual staff
configuration files. These are treated as hard constraints and cannot
be overridden by staffing minimums. If minimum staffing cannot be met
due to simultaneous absences, the system will flag an infeasibility.

SWEDISH WORK LAW RULES — MEDICAL ROSTERING SYSTEM
====================================================
Source: Arbetsmiljöverket (Swedish Work Environment Authority)
        Arbetstidslagen (1982:673) — Working Hours Act
        AFS 2005:6 — Provisions on systematic work environment management
        AFS 1982:17 — Night work in healthcare

NOTE: Healthcare employers are bound by both statutory law and collective
agreements (kollektivavtal). Where a collective agreement is more restrictive,
it takes precedence. The rules below reflect statutory minimums.

Reference: https://www.av.se/arbetstid/


SECTION 1 — DAILY WORKING HOURS
---------------------------------
Rule 1.1 — Standard daily working time:
  Maximum 8 hours per day for a normal working day.
  A working day is counted from the start of one shift to the start of the next.

Rule 1.2 — Minimum daily rest (dygnsvila):
  At least 11 consecutive hours of rest must be provided between the end of
  one shift and the start of the next shift.
  Basis: Arbetstidslagen §13 and EU Working Time Directive 2003/88/EC Article 3.

Rule 1.3 — Mandatory break (rast):
  For any shift exceeding 5 hours, at least 30 minutes of uninterrupted break
  must be provided. Break is not counted as working time.

Implication for shift scheduling (08:00-shift model):
  SHIFT TIMES used in this department:
    Day     (D): 07:00 – 15:00  (8 h)
    Evening (E): 15:00 – 23:00  (8 h)
    Night   (N): 23:00 – 07:00  (8 h, ends following calendar day)

  FORBIDDEN CONSECUTIVE SHIFT TRANSITIONS (violate 11 h rest rule):
    Evening shift → Day shift next day    (gap = 8 h  < 11 h — FORBIDDEN)
    Night shift   → Day shift next day    (gap = 0 h         — FORBIDDEN)
    Night shift   → Evening shift next day (gap = 8 h < 11 h — FORBIDDEN)

  PERMITTED CONSECUTIVE TRANSITIONS:
    Day     → Evening same day             (gap = 0 h — prevented by 1-shift/day rule)
    Day     → Any shift next day           (gap ≥ 16 h — OK)
    Evening → Evening or Night next day    (gap ≥ 16 h — OK)
    Night   → Night next day               (gap = 16 h — OK)


SECTION 2 — WEEKLY WORKING HOURS
----------------------------------
Rule 2.1 — Maximum weekly working time:
  Ordinary working time must not exceed 40 hours per week on average.
  Can be averaged over a reference period of up to 4 weeks.
  Basis: Arbetstidslagen §10.

Rule 2.2 — Maximum working time over 4-week period:
  The 4-week rolling average must not exceed 40 hours/week → 160 hours per 4 weeks.
  In exceptional cases (overtime), up to 48 hours per week may apply temporarily,
  but the average over the reference period must not exceed 40 h/week.

Rule 2.3 — Weekly rest (veckovila):
  Every worker is entitled to at least 36 consecutive hours of rest per 7-day period.
  This rest period should, where possible, include Sunday.
  Basis: Arbetstidslagen §14.

Implication for scheduling:
  With 8-hour shifts, max 5 shifts per week (5 × 8 = 40 h).
  Any 7-day window must contain at least 2 consecutive days off to ensure 36 h rest.
  Hard rule: no more than 5 working days in any 7-day rolling window.
  Hard rule: no more than 5 consecutive working days without a day off.


SECTION 3 — OVERTIME
---------------------
Rule 3.1 — Overtime limits:
  Maximum overtime per calendar month:  50 hours
  Maximum overtime per calendar year:   200 hours
  Overtime must be agreed and documented.
  Basis: Arbetstidslagen §8.

Rule 3.2 — Overtime compensation:
  Overtime must be compensated with extra pay or compensatory leave (kompledighet).
  This system does not schedule overtime unless explicitly allowed.


SECTION 4 — NIGHT WORK (NATTARBETE)
-------------------------------------
Rule 4.1 — Definition of night time:
  Night time (nattetid) is defined as the period between 22:00 and 06:00.
  Basis: Arbetstidslagen §13a.

  In this department: the Night shift (23:00–07:00) falls within night time.
  The Evening shift (15:00–23:00) overlaps night time by 1 hour.

Rule 4.2 — Night worker classification:
  A worker is a "night worker" (nattarbetare) if they regularly work at least
  3 hours of their normal working time during the night period, or if at least
  one-third of their annual working time falls during night time.

Rule 4.3 — Maximum for night workers:
  Night workers must not work more than an average of 8 hours per 24-hour period,
  calculated over a reference period of 4 months.
  Basis: Arbetstidslagen §13b.

Rule 4.4 — Health assessments for night workers:
  Night workers are entitled to a health assessment before assignment and
  periodically thereafter. The employer must offer this assessment.

Implication for scheduling:
  Night shifts in this system are 8 hours. With max 1 shift per day, the 8 h/24 h
  limit is automatically satisfied per shift. Monitor total night shift proportion
  over 4-week blocks to ensure the 1/3 annual threshold is not inadvertently exceeded.
  Recommended: no more than 8 night shifts per 4-week block per worker.


SECTION 5 — HEALTHCARE-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS
------------------------------------------------
Rule 5.1 — Continuous operations (kontinuerlig verksamhet):
  Healthcare facilities classified as continuous operations may deviate from
  certain weekly rest rules through collective agreements. However, workers
  must always receive the 11-hour daily rest (Section 1 Rule 1.2).

Rule 5.2 — On-call (beredskap):
  On-call time where the employee is available but not required to be present
  is not counted as working time under Arbetstidslagen §6a.
  Active on-call (standby at the workplace) is counted as working time.

Rule 5.3 — Psychosocial work environment:
  AFS 2015:4 (Organisational and Social Work Environment) requires that
  work schedules do not create unhealthy stress or social isolation.
  Rostering should avoid excessive consecutive night shifts and provide
  predictable schedules with reasonable notice (minimum 2 weeks).

Rule 5.4 — Individual consideration:
  Employees with documented medical conditions, pregnancy, or caregiving
  obligations may have additional rights to adjusted scheduling.
  These must be handled as mandatory (hard) constraints.


SECTION 6 — SYSTEM CONSTRAINT SUMMARY (for solver configuration)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The following hard constraints are derived from the above rules:

HC-01  At most 1 shift per employee per calendar day.
HC-02  Forbidden transition: Evening(d) → Day(d+1)   [11 h rest]
HC-03  Forbidden transition: Night(d) → Day(d+1)     [11 h rest]
HC-04  Forbidden transition: Night(d) → Evening(d+1) [11 h rest]
HC-05  At most 5 shifts per employee per 7-day week.
HC-06  At most 5 working days in any rolling 6-day window (no 6 consecutive days).
HC-07  Maximum 8 night shifts per employee per 4-week block.
HC-08  Contract hours: weekly shifts capped at ceiling(contract_pct × 5).

Soft constraints (solver will maximize compliance, not guarantee):

SC-01  Preferred shift types per employee.
SC-02  Preferred days off per employee.
SC-03  Avoid specified shift types per employee.
SC-04  Maximum night shifts per week per employee.
SC-05  Minimize consecutive shifts of the same unfavored type.
